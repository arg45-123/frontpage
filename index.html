Welcome to the Drinking Water Survey

Water is essential for life, and ensuring its quality is a fundamental responsibility. This survey was conducted to assess the drinking water quality and infrastructure at PRMIT&R, providing insights into both quantitative and qualitative aspects.

Introduction

We are a group of 4 students - Arpan, Aditya, Pranav, and Yash - conducting a survey under the Project. Our primary objective was to analyze the drinking water facilities available in our college, focusing on both qualitative and quantitative aspects.

Our survey included visiting multiple departments, collecting data on water sources, and identifying any issues with water coolers, storage tanks, and water quality. We aimed to assess whether the available drinking water is sufficient, clean, and properly maintained.

This project was carried out under the guidance of faculties., ensuring the authenticity and reliability of the collected data.

Methodology

To ensure accurate results, we divided our methodology into two major sections: Quantitative Analysis and Qualitative Analysis.

Qualitative Analysis includes various water tests such pH, TDS, chlorides etc and Quantitative Analysis include parameters such as water sources, water coolers,tanks , refiiling frequency etc.

Conclusion & Recommendations

Based on our findings, we suggest the following improvements:

  • Regular water quality testing should be conducted to ensure safe drinking water.
  • Malfunctioning water coolers should be repaired or replaced.
  • Periodic cleaning of storage tanks should be strictly followed.
  • Students should be educated on water conservation and hygiene.

Through this survey, we have gained valuable insights into the water infrastructure of our college and have provided actionable recommendations to enhance its quality.

Future Scope

1. Regular Monitoring and Trend Analysis

Conduct periodic water quality tests (monthly or quarterly) to observe variations in pH, TDS, hardness, and microbial contamination. Establish a trend analysis system to predict potential issues before they become critical.

2. Incorporating Advanced Testing Methods

Introduce real-time water quality monitoring sensors for continuous assessment. Expand the survey to include microbiological tests to detect harmful bacteria and pathogens.

3. Evaluating the Impact of Seasonal Changes

Conduct comparative studies of water quality in different seasons to assess fluctuations due to rainfall, temperature, and groundwater levels.Analyze how monsoons, summers, and winters affect the borewell water quality and overall supply system.

4. Integration with Digital Platforms

Develop an online dashboard or mobile app where students and faculty can report water quality concerns.Use data visualization techniques to present real-time water quality statistics for better decision-making.
